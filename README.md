## Mindset

- Be relaxed, but not nonchalant.
- Play the ball.
- Think about what you _want_, not what you don't want.
- Stay loose.
- Feel nothing.
    - You're not trying to impress anyone.
- It's just you and the ball.

## Footwork

- Move your feet. All the time.
- Lots of little steps.
- Stay on your toes. Avoid flat-footedness.

## Serve

- Clear your mind.
- Follow the same ritual, the exact same way, every time.
- Decide on type of serveâ€”and don't change your mind.
- Focus on execution (trajectory to target zone) not outcome (e.g. forcing an error).
    - Focus on angle, direction, height over net, spin, and pace.
- Make sure you take enough time.
    - Don't rush.
    - Try: Take a deep breath.
    - Try: Count to 5.
- Don't futz with your grip.
    - Grip is insignificant compared to seeing the ball.
- Serve decisively.
- "Throw" the racket.
- You don't need to swing hard. Think natural but decisive.
- Don't drop your head!
- Is your arm extending fully?
- Are your shoulders slanted enough?
- Did you reach trophy pose quickly enough?
- Did you really _see_ the ball before you swung?
- Can you _see_ the racket pass through the ball?
- Did your body rotate?
- Did you fully commit?
- Did you fully release?
- Shoulder-based swing. Not elbow.
- "Elbow the enemy."
- Did you pull the racket all the way back?
- Was the racket head "up" soon enough?

### Ball toss

- Did you toss the ball high enough?
- Did you toss the ball out in front tenough?
- Did your toss arm really extend?
- Try breathing out as you toss.
- Try tossing a bit slower, smoother.

## Groundstrokes

- Ready stance.
- Move your feet.
- Bend your knees.
- Stay in the toaster.
- Step in.
- Brush the ball from 6 o'clock to 12 o'clock.
- Throw the racket.

### Forehand

- Finish with racket slung over shoulder.
- Your non-dominant arm should:
    - Hold the ball at bay.
    - Keep you stady and counterbalance your swing.

## Volley

- Athletic stance. Knees bent. Leaning forward.
- Think "hungry hungry hippo." Go after it.
- Be moving forward.
- Track the ball like a cyborg.
- Don't wince.

### Punch volley

- Think boxing. Hands out in front.
- You should be able to see the back of the strings.
- Your head should follow the racket.
- Try: "Strike a pose." Freeze just after contact.
- Try: Your body's momentum gives it force.
- Try: Step at contact.

### Swing volley

- Be stepping toward your target.

## Smash

- When you see a high lob, think "This is my favorite shot. I can't wait to smash this."
- Send a message: "I won't let you lob me without consequences."
- Get behind the ball. (Or: Keep the ball in front of you.)
- Keep moving your feet until _just before_ you strike. Don't settle too soon.
- Don't drop your head! See it all the way down.
- Fully commit. Full swing, body rotation, and release.
